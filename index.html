Timeline of the Napoleonic Era

  • 15 August 1769 - Napoleon Bonaparte was born in Ajaccio, Corsica
  • 28 October 1785 - Napoleon graduates from Ecole Militaire with the rank of second lieutenant in the artillery
  • 2 March 1796 - Napoleon is given command of the French army in Italy
  • 5 December 1797 - Napoleon returns to Paris as a hero
  • 12 December 1799 - Napoleon elected First Consul of the Consulate
  • 1 May 1802 - Napoleon restructures French educational system
  • 18 May 1803 - Britain declares war on France
  • 24 March 1804 - Introduction of the Civil Code (also known as Napoleon Code)
  • 18 May 1804 - Napoleon proclaimed Emperor of the French by the Senate
  • 2 December 1804 - Napoleon crowns himself emperor, in the company of the Pope
  • 15 September 1806 - Prussia joins Britain and Russia against Napoleon
  • 14 September 1812 - Napoleon arrives in Moscow to find the city abandoned and set alight by the inhabitants; retreating in the midst of a frigid winter, the army suffers great losses
  • 4 April 1814 - Napoleon abdicates his rule and Louis XVIII, a Bourbon, is restored to the French throne
  • 4 May 1814 - Napoleon is exiled to Elba; his wife and son take refuge in Vienna
  • 18 June 1815 - Napoleon is defeated in the Battle of Waterloo
